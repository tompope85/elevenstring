# PRIMER: elevenstring

## What this is for
We all carry beliefs about how the world works and how it should. Most of them we have never said out loud, but they decide what we notice, how we act, and what we take for granted. There's nothing wrong with this. It's human. Without the ability to operate under assumptions, we would never get anywhere.

The trouble comes when we are unaware of how they're affecting us and others in a given context, and whether and how we should update them. The less aware or capable we are in this, the less potential we have to shape our world and share our meaning.

elevenstring exists so we can open up more possibilities in the moment, and learn from them after the fact.

It provides a sanctuary for your inner world, in relation to the outside world. What makes something meaningful to you is yours forever, and at the same time you have pathways to evaluate whether what was meaningful once is meaningful now.

The information you need to make your own choices is yours. And the reasoning behind those choices remains observable to you alone, with its full provenance preserved.

## What a framing is made of
elevenstring calls your whole set of beliefs, stated and unstated, a framing. Every framing rests on the same fixed core, the kernel. That shared floor lets any two meet without one swallowing the other. Above it sits everything you set yourself. What exists, what's true, who acts, which lenses to weigh them by. The machinery is in SPEC. What matters here is the moment yours runs into one built differently.

## The triad
Three markers for typing any relation between you and whatever you meet:

- **All.** It maps cleanly onto your framing. You take it in as a version of what's already yours. Most tools and methods inside a tradition type as all.
- **Other.** It is genuinely different, and you stay in relation with it without swallowing it or throwing it out. Zen non-dualism or hard determinism might type as other to a Western analytic framing.
- **Nothing.** No relation is possible here. It sits outside engagement.

The triad makes no claim about reality. It types considerations, not truths. Calling something other doesn't mean it is absolutely different. Right now, from where you stand, you treat it as other. With more contact, that can change.

This is what the whole thing turns on. You don't have to colonise what you meet, and you don't have to dismiss it. You name the relation, let it stand, and carry on with the difference in view. A difference you can see is one you can work with: to keep yourself honest, or to say plainly where you and someone else diverge.

## A worked example
You read a book on Buddhist non-dualism. Your current framing runs a process ontology, coherence-plus-ownership for truth, and project-oriented agency. Typed layer by layer:

- Ontology: you both reject substance, but your positive accounts diverge → other, with overlap
- Truth: yours is propositional, theirs non-propositional direct realisation → other
- Agency: yours assumes intentional projects, theirs their cessation → other
- Aggregate: other, at the layers that matter most

You don't have to adopt the position or reject it. You keep a clear record of it, typed and linked to your other Buddhist-philosophy encounters, and you carry on aware of the difference. Take up meditation later and you can revisit it, re-typing from lived experience rather than reading, in a new record that points back to this one.
