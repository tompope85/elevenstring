# PRIMER: elevenstring

## What this is for
We move through the world with our own sets of commitments. Whether explicitly, implicitly, rigidly or flexibly, we all carry a framing for how things are. How we see ourselves and how we want things to be.

What counts as true. What kinds of things exist. Where the line falls between ourselves and others. What makes a decision right. These heuristics, for better or worse, guide us. They shape what we notice, how we act, and what we take for granted.

They stay mostly invisible. It might only be when we're confronted with another framework, in a certain context, that we become aware of our own. A person, an AI, a domain, or a tradition that doesn't share yours, and the framing shows.

These moments of recognition can be confronting in the moment, but sometimes, on reflection, they liberate us.

But often they just end in a battle for one to assimilate the other. They steamroll us, or us them. Or one of us passively lets it go. Or we react in a way that doesn't feel like us. Or we feel like we're compromising, but we're not even sure what the compromise is. And these moments accrue. One way or another, they become a cost to be paid by someone.

First week at a new job, you suggest the obvious fix, and the room goes quiet. The doctor says it's stress; your body says otherwise. Legal and engineering both call it "done," and mean two different months. You export your data into a new tool, and half the fields quietly don't survive. An assistant echoes your thoughts back at you, then contaminates something in the small print.

elevenstring is a different way. Your framing is your framing. And yours alone. But you operate with a scaffolding that helps you adapt that framing dynamically and cooperatively, in pursuit of contextual values.

When you come across a situation or context that your framing can't resolve, you have a structured process adapted to *your* preferences, your strengths, your uncertainties about the situation, so *you* can make the informed choice about what fits where.

## What a framing is made of
Your framing rests on a fixed core, the kernel: the part every framing shares, so that two can meet without one swallowing the other. Above it is everything you set yourself: what you hold about what exists, what's true, who acts, and the lenses you weigh it by. The machinery is in SPEC. What matters here is what happens when your framing meets one built differently.

## The triad
Three markers for typing any relation between you and whatever you meet:

- **All.** It maps cleanly onto your framing. You take it in as a version of what you already hold. Most tools and methods inside a tradition type as all.
- **Other.** It is genuinely different, and you stay in relation with it. You hold it as different instead of swallowing it or throwing it out. Zen non-dualism or hard determinism might type as other to a Western analytic framing.
- **Nothing.** No relation is possible here. It sits outside engagement.

The triad makes no claim about reality. It types considerations, not truths. Calling something other doesn't mean it is absolutely different. It means that right now, from where you stand, you treat it as other. With more contact, that can change.

This is what the whole thing turns on. You don't have to colonise what you meet, and you don't have to dismiss it. You name the relation, hold it as it is, and carry on with the difference in view. A difference you can see is one you can work with: to keep yourself honest, or to say plainly where you and someone else diverge.

## A worked example
(See [EXAMPLES.md](./EXAMPLES.md) for more.)

You read a book on Buddhist non-dualism. Your current framing runs a process ontology, coherence-plus-ownership for truth, and project-oriented agency. Typed layer by layer:

- Ontology: you both reject substance, but your positive accounts diverge → other, with overlap
- Truth: yours is propositional, theirs non-propositional direct realisation → other
- Agency: yours assumes intentional projects, theirs their cessation → other
- Aggregate: other, at the layers that matter most

You don't have to adopt the position or reject it. You hold a clear record of it, typed and linked to your other Buddhist-philosophy encounters, and you carry on aware of the difference. Take up meditation later and you can revisit it, re-typing from lived experience rather than reading, in a new record that points back to this one.
